A Summary of Figurative Language in "The Drummer Boy Of Shiloh" by Ray Bradbury and "A Drummer-Boy’s Diary" by William Bircher

Figurative Language Phrase Explanation
"...a hundred thousand men… lay crazily askew in their uniform” (Metaphor) Men are compared to carelessly strewn objects as they are laid across the battlefield.
Peach Blossoms (Symbolism) The peach blossom symbolizes youth, fragility, and innocence that the younger soldiers have.
“You are the heart of the army. Think of that. You’re the heart of the army” (Metaphor) Joby is told to be the heart of the army which means that he sets the pace for the battle just like a heart setting the pace for blood pumping.
"...Kentucky might be made to blossom as the rose.” (Metaphor) The narrator compares the potential of Kentucky to the flourishing of a beautiful flower.
"We were here loaded on transports and packed in like sardines…” (Simile) The narrator compares the crowded transport vehicles to the way sardines are packed into a can.
"... the cold wind coming down the river seemed to chill us to the marrow.” (Hyperbole) The narrator’s exaggeration is used to convey the intense cold the soldiers feel.
